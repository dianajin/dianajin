Martin Luther King Jr. (born Michael King Jr., January 15, 1929 – April 4, 1968) was an American Baptist minister and activist who was a leader in the Civil Rights Movement. He is best known for his role in the advancement of civil rights using nonviolent civil disobedience based on his Christian beliefs.

Here is a brief timeline of Martin Luther King Jr's life:

Year Event
1929 Michael King Jr was born in Atlanta, Georgia.
1934 Michael King's name was changed to Martin Luther, named after German reformer Martin Luther.
1947 King joins the ministry.
1954 King is called as a pastor in Montgomery, Alabama.
1957 King helps found the Southern Christian Leadership Conference.
1961 King is called as a pastor in Montgomery, Alabama.
1963 During a campaign against racial segregation and economic injustice in Birmingham, Alabama, King is arrested and composes a letter from the Birmingham Jail.
1963 Martin Luther King Jr, representing the SCLC, along with the other "Big Six" civil right organizations organizes a March on Washing for Jobs and Freedom.
1965 King organizes a march to Selma following a previous march aborted by Bloody Sunday.
1968 Martin Luther King Jr is shot and killed in his motel.
1968 The Civil Rights Act of 1968 is passed.
1983 President Reagan signs legislation creating Martin Luther King Jr. Day.
2000 Utah recognizes Martin Luther King Jr. Day by name (after renaming their Human Rights Day state holiday), officially making Martin Luther King Jr. Day celebrated in all states.

King was posthumously awarded the Presidential Medal of Freedom and the Congressional Gold Medal. Martin Luther King Jr. Day was established as a holiday in numerous cities and states beginning in 1971, and as a U.S. federal holiday in 1986. Hundreds of streets in the U.S. have been renamed in his honor, and a county in Washington State was also renamed for him. The Martin Luther King Jr. Memorial on the National Mall in Washington, D.C., was dedicated in 2011.
